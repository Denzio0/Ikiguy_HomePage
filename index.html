What is an Ikigai?

Ikigai is a Japanese concept that translates to "a reason for being." It combines the terms "iki," meaning "alive" or "life," and "gai," meaning "benefit" or "worth." Ikigai represents the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for, helping individuals discover their purpose and lead a meaningful life.

Passion

Overlap of what you love and what you are good at. Focus on your skills and hobbies.

Mission

Overlap of what you love and what the world needs. Align your purpose with social impact.

Profession

Overlap of what you are good at and what you can be paid for. Focus on sustainable career skills.

Vocation

Overlap of what the world needs and what you can be paid for. Aim for meaningful impact in your profession.

Mental Health Challenges Among Filipino Youth

According to the Philippine Mental Health Association (PMHA), young Filipinos and Generation Z face various mental health issues. These conditions impact emotional well-being, daily performance, and social relationships. The statistics below show prevalence rates and key characteristics of these challenges.

Anxiety

Affects 28% of Filipino youth. Includes excessive worry, nervousness, and fear that disrupts daily activities.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Depression

Experienced by 22% of youth, characterized by persistent sadness, low energy, and lack of interest in previously enjoyed activities.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Stress

About 35% of Filipino students report chronic stress from school, work, or family responsibilities.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Low Self-Esteem

Affects 30% of teens, leading to self-doubt, negative self-image, and social withdrawal.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Coping Strategies for Mental Well-Being

According to the Philippine Mental Health Association (PMHA), using healthy coping strategies helps manage stress, anxiety, and other mental health challenges. Explore these practical methods for daily emotional resilience.

Mindfulness

Practice awareness of the present moment to reduce stress and improve focus.

Source: Jon Kabat-Zinn, 1990

Exercise

Engage in physical activity to release endorphins, improve mood, and reduce anxiety.

Source: APA, 2023

Social Support

Connect with friends, family, or mentors for guidance and emotional encouragement.

Source: Nielsen & Schrøder, 2023

Journaling

Record thoughts and emotions daily to track patterns and reduce mental clutter.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Meditation

Practice guided or silent meditation for at least 10 minutes daily to improve focus and calm the mind.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Breathing Exercises

Deep breathing techniques reduce anxiety and help regulate the nervous system. Effective for 25% of youth experiencing stress.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Creative Arts

Engage in painting, music, or crafts to express emotions and reduce stress. Proven to improve mood in 30% of young adults.

Source: PMHA, 2024

Time Management

Plan daily tasks and prioritize activities to reduce overwhelm. Effective in lowering perceived stress for 27% of students.

Source: PMHA, 2024
